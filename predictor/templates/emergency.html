Severe Burn — Critical Response Guide

Call Emergency ServicesDial 108 immediately. Third-degree burns are life-threatening and require instant hospital care.

No Water, Ice or OintmentsDo NOT apply water, ice, cream, or butter — this worsens injury and can cause shock.

Cover with Clean ClothLoosely cover with a sterile dry dressing. Never wrap tightly or use adhesive bandages.

Keep Patient Calm & WarmLay the person flat, keep warm with a blanket. Shock can develop rapidly.

Monitor BreathingBurns near the face can swell the airway. Watch for hoarseness or difficulty breathing.

No Food or DrinkDo not give the patient anything to eat or drink — surgery may be required immediately.
